Overview

This repository contains the official code, documentation, and implementation for the research paper titled "Comparative Analysis of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Traditional Models for Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA)." The study evaluates and contrasts the performance of LLMs like Flan-T5 and DeBERTa with traditional models like LSTM in the context of ABSA.

Research Objectives

Systematic comparison of modern LLMs vs traditional models for ABSA across multiple domains and datasets.

  • Fine-tuned LLMs vs LSTM models
  • Prompt-engineering effectiveness
  • Cross-domain performance analysis
  • Computational efficiency trade-offs

Key Contributions

Novel insights into ABSA model performance and practical implementation guidelines.

  • First systematic LLM vs traditional comparison
  • Multi-domain evaluation framework
  • Dual approach analysis (fine-tuning + prompting)
  • Cross-domain generalization study

Model Architectures

Large Language Models

  • Flan-T5: 248M parameters, instruction-following
  • DeBERTa: 140M parameters, disentangled attention
  • BART: 140M parameters, seq2seq architecture
  • GPT-3.5/4: 175B+ parameters, prompt-engineering

Traditional Models

  • BiLSTM: Bidirectional LSTM with attention
  • LSTM Variants: Standard and attention-based
  • Cross-Domain LSTM: Domain adaptation models
  • Custom Architectures: ABSA-specific designs
